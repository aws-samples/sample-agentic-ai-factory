# Business Feasibility Assessment Guidelines

**Weight:** 25%  
**Description:** Evaluates organizational readiness, value alignment, and change management capabilities for agentic AI transformation

## Assessment Categories

### 1. Business Objectives & Value Alignment

**Points to Extract:**
- Primary business objectives for AI transformation
- Expected business outcomes and success metrics
- Strategic alignment with organizational goals
- Problem statements and pain points being addressed
- Competitive pressures or market drivers
- Innovation vs efficiency focus

**Sample Questions:**
- What business problems are you trying to solve with agentic AI?
- What does success look like for this transformation?
- How does this align with your broader strategic objectives?
- What competitive or market pressures are driving this initiative?
- Are you focused more on innovation or operational efficiency?

### 2. Stakeholder Engagement & Buy-in

**Points to Extract:**
- Executive sponsorship level and commitment
- Key stakeholder identification and involvement
- Cross-functional alignment and support
- Budget authority and decision-making power
- Resistance or concerns from stakeholders
- Communication and engagement strategy

**Sample Questions:**
- Who is the executive sponsor for this initiative?
- Which departments and stakeholders are involved?
- Do you have buy-in from all affected business units?
- Are there any concerns or resistance you're encountering?
- How are you communicating about this transformation?

### 3. Organizational Culture & Innovation

**Points to Extract:**
- Innovation appetite and risk tolerance
- Previous transformation experience and outcomes
- Learning culture and experimentation mindset
- Failure tolerance and psychological safety
- Speed of decision-making and bureaucracy level
- Collaboration across teams and silos

**Sample Questions:**
- How would you describe your organization's appetite for innovation?
- Have you undertaken major technology transformations before?
- How does your organization handle failure and learning?
- How quickly can decisions be made and implemented?
- How well do teams collaborate across organizational boundaries?

### 4. User Adoption & Change Readiness

**Points to Extract:**
- End user identification and segmentation
- Current user technology proficiency
- User attitudes toward AI and automation
- Change fatigue or transformation saturation
- User involvement in design and testing
- Training and support infrastructure

**Sample Questions:**
- Who are the primary users of the agentic AI solution?
- How comfortable are users with current technology?
- What's the general attitude toward AI and automation?
- Are users experiencing change fatigue from other initiatives?
- How will users be involved in the design process?
- What training and support will be provided?

### 5. Change Management Capability

**Points to Extract:**
- Change management methodology and maturity
- Change management resources and expertise
- Communication plan and channels
- Training program design and delivery approach
- Resistance management strategies
- Success measurement and feedback loops

**Sample Questions:**
- Do you have a change management team or methodology?
- What's your approach to managing organizational change?
- How will you communicate changes to affected users?
- What training programs are planned?
- How will you measure adoption and success?
- How will you gather and act on user feedback?

### 6. Process Maturity & Automation

**Points to Extract:**
- Current process documentation and standardization
- Existing automation level and tools
- Process optimization and continuous improvement culture
- Workflow complexity and variability
- Exception handling and edge case frequency
- Process ownership and accountability

**Sample Questions:**
- How well documented and standardized are your processes?
- What level of automation do you currently have?
- Do you have a continuous improvement culture?
- How complex and variable are the workflows you want to automate?
- How often do exceptions and edge cases occur?
- Who owns and is accountable for these processes?

### 7. Skills & Capabilities

**Points to Extract:**
- Current team skills and competencies
- AI/ML expertise availability
- Skill gaps and training needs
- Hiring plans and talent acquisition strategy
- Partner and vendor relationships
- Knowledge transfer and retention approach

**Sample Questions:**
- What AI/ML skills exist in your current team?
- What skill gaps have you identified?
- Are you planning to hire or train existing staff?
- Do you have partnerships with AI vendors or consultants?
- How do you ensure knowledge transfer and retention?

### 8. Success Metrics & Measurement

**Points to Extract:**
- Key performance indicators (KPIs) defined
- Baseline metrics and current state measurement
- Target metrics and success thresholds
- Measurement frequency and reporting cadence
- Analytics and reporting infrastructure
- Continuous improvement and iteration approach

**Sample Questions:**
- What KPIs will you use to measure success?
- Do you have baseline metrics for current performance?
- What targets are you aiming to achieve?
- How often will you measure and report progress?
- Do you have analytics infrastructure to track these metrics?
- How will you iterate based on results?

## Scoring Guidance

**High Readiness:** Strong executive sponsorship, clear value alignment, innovation culture, mature change management, engaged users, defined success metrics

**Medium Readiness:** Management support, reasonable alignment, some change capability, moderate user engagement, basic metrics defined

**Low Readiness:** Weak sponsorship, unclear value proposition, resistant culture, no change management, user concerns, undefined success criteria
