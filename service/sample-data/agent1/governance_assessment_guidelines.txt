Governance, Risk & Compliance Assessment Guidelines

Weight: 25%
Description: Evaluates organizational governance structures, risk management capabilities, and compliance requirements for autonomous AI systems

Assessment Categories

1. AI Governance Framework

Points to Extract:
- Existing AI/ML governance policies and structures
- AI ethics board or oversight committee existence
- Decision-making authority for AI deployments
- AI risk assessment processes
- Model approval and validation workflows
- Responsible AI principles and guidelines

Sample Questions:
- Do you have an AI governance framework or policy in place?
- Is there an AI ethics board or oversight committee?
- Who has authority to approve AI system deployments?
- How do you assess risks associated with AI systems?
- What responsible AI principles does your organization follow?

2. Regulatory & Compliance Requirements

Points to Extract:
- Industry-specific regulations (HIPAA, PCI-DSS, SOX, GDPR, etc.)
- Data privacy requirements and jurisdictions
- AI-specific regulations (EU AI Act, state-level AI laws)
- Audit and reporting requirements
- Data retention and deletion policies
- Cross-border data transfer restrictions

Sample Questions:
- What regulatory frameworks apply to your organization?
- Do you operate in jurisdictions with specific AI regulations?
- What data privacy laws must you comply with?
- What are your audit and reporting obligations?
- Do you have restrictions on cross-border data transfers?

3. Risk Management

Points to Extract:
- Enterprise risk management framework maturity
- Risk appetite for autonomous decision-making
- Risk assessment methodologies in use
- Risk mitigation and contingency planning processes
- Insurance coverage for AI-related risks
- Third-party risk management approach

Sample Questions:
- How mature is your enterprise risk management framework?
- What's your organization's appetite for AI making autonomous decisions?
- How do you currently assess and mitigate technology risks?
- Do you have insurance coverage for AI-related incidents?
- How do you manage risks from third-party vendors and services?

4. Data Governance

Points to Extract:
- Data ownership and stewardship model
- Data classification and labeling practices
- Data lineage and provenance tracking
- Data quality management processes
- Master data management approach
- Data access controls and policies

Sample Questions:
- Who owns and is accountable for different data domains?
- How do you classify and label sensitive data?
- Can you track data lineage and provenance?
- What processes ensure data quality?
- How do you control access to sensitive data?

5. Model Governance & Explainability

Points to Extract:
- Model documentation and registry practices
- Model versioning and change management
- Explainability and interpretability requirements
- Bias detection and mitigation processes
- Model performance monitoring and drift detection
- Model decommissioning procedures

Sample Questions:
- How do you document and track AI models in production?
- What are your requirements for model explainability?
- How do you detect and mitigate bias in AI systems?
- Do you monitor models for performance drift?
- What's your process for retiring or replacing models?

6. Audit & Traceability

Points to Extract:
- Audit logging requirements and retention
- Decision traceability and explainability needs
- Compliance reporting capabilities
- Immutable audit trail requirements
- Right to explanation obligations
- Audit frequency and scope

Sample Questions:
- What audit logging is required for your systems?
- Do you need to explain AI-driven decisions to customers or regulators?
- How often are you audited and by whom?
- Do you need immutable audit trails?
- What compliance reports must you generate?

7. Security Governance

Points to Extract:
- Security policies and standards
- Security certification requirements (SOC 2, ISO 27001, etc.)
- Vulnerability management processes
- Penetration testing and security assessment frequency
- Incident response and breach notification procedures
- Security training and awareness programs

Sample Questions:
- What security certifications does your organization hold or require?
- How often do you conduct security assessments and penetration tests?
- What's your incident response and breach notification process?
- Do you have security training programs for employees?
- What security standards must vendors and partners meet?

8. Change Management & Approval

Points to Extract:
- Change approval board structure and authority
- Change management process maturity
- Deployment approval requirements
- Rollback and emergency change procedures
- Stakeholder notification and communication processes
- Change impact assessment practices

Sample Questions:
- What's your change approval process for production systems?
- Who must approve AI system deployments?
- How do you assess the impact of changes?
- What are your rollback procedures?
- How do you communicate changes to stakeholders?

Scoring Guidance

High Readiness: Mature AI governance framework, comprehensive compliance program, strong risk management, established audit processes, clear accountability

Medium Readiness: Basic governance policies, standard compliance practices, developing risk framework, some audit capabilities, defined roles

Low Readiness: No AI governance, reactive compliance approach, limited risk management, minimal audit trails, unclear accountability
