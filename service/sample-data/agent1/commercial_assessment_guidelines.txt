Commercial & Economics Assessment Guidelines

Weight: 20%
Description: Evaluates financial viability, budget adequacy, cost modeling, and return on investment for agentic AI transformation

Assessment Categories

1. Budget & Investment

Points to Extract:
- Total budget allocated for transformation
- Budget breakdown (development, infrastructure, training, operations)
- Budget approval status and authority
- Multi-year investment commitment
- Contingency and reserve allocation
- Funding source (CapEx, OpEx, innovation fund)

Sample Questions:
- What budget has been allocated for this transformation?
- How is the budget distributed across different areas?
- Has the budget been formally approved?
- Is this a one-time investment or multi-year commitment?
- Do you have contingency funds for unexpected costs?
- How is this being funded (CapEx, OpEx, etc.)?

2. Cost Modeling & Estimation

Points to Extract:
- Cost estimation methodology and accuracy
- Understanding of AI/ML cost drivers (tokens, compute, storage)
- Infrastructure cost projections
- Licensing and subscription costs
- Professional services and consulting budget
- Hidden or overlooked cost considerations

Sample Questions:
- How did you arrive at your budget estimates?
- Do you understand the cost drivers for AI systems (tokens, compute)?
- What infrastructure costs are you anticipating?
- Have you budgeted for licenses, subscriptions, and professional services?
- What costs might you be overlooking?

3. Operational Costs & Sustainability

Points to Extract:
- Ongoing operational cost projections
- Model inference and API call volume estimates
- Data storage and transfer costs
- Monitoring and observability costs
- Support and maintenance budget
- Cost optimization strategies and FinOps practices

Sample Questions:
- What are your projected ongoing operational costs?
- What volume of AI model calls do you expect?
- Have you estimated data storage and transfer costs?
- What's your budget for monitoring and support?
- Do you have FinOps practices or cost optimization strategies?
- How will you control and optimize costs over time?

4. Return on Investment (ROI)

Points to Extract:
- Expected ROI and payback period
- ROI calculation methodology
- Quantifiable benefits (cost savings, revenue increase)
- Qualitative benefits (customer satisfaction, competitive advantage)
- ROI measurement and tracking approach
- Sensitivity analysis and risk scenarios

Sample Questions:
- What ROI are you expecting from this transformation?
- What's your target payback period?
- How are you calculating ROI?
- What quantifiable benefits do you expect (cost savings, revenue)?
- What qualitative benefits are you targeting?
- How will you measure and track ROI over time?

5. Resource Allocation

Points to Extract:
- Team size and composition
- Full-time vs contractor/consultant mix
- Resource availability and allocation percentage
- Competing priorities and resource conflicts
- Backfill or replacement plans for allocated resources
- Ramp-up and ramp-down resource planning

Sample Questions:
- How many people will be dedicated to this initiative?
- What's the mix of full-time staff vs contractors?
- What percentage of time can team members dedicate?
- Are there competing priorities for these resources?
- Do you have backfill plans for allocated staff?
- How will you scale the team up and down?

6. Cost-Benefit Analysis

Points to Extract:
- Current state costs (baseline)
- Efficiency gains and productivity improvements
- Error reduction and quality improvements
- Time savings and speed to market
- Customer experience improvements
- Risk reduction and compliance cost avoidance

Sample Questions:
- What are your current costs for the processes being transformed?
- What efficiency gains do you expect?
- How will this reduce errors or improve quality?
- What time savings or speed improvements do you anticipate?
- How will customer experience improve?
- What risks or compliance costs will be reduced?

7. Financial Governance & Controls

Points to Extract:
- Budget tracking and reporting processes
- Variance analysis and corrective action procedures
- Approval thresholds and escalation paths
- Cost allocation and chargeback models
- Financial audit and compliance requirements
- Budget flexibility and reallocation authority

Sample Questions:
- How will you track and report on budget vs actuals?
- What's your process for handling budget variances?
- What are the approval thresholds for additional spending?
- Do you have cost allocation or chargeback models?
- What financial audit requirements apply?
- How much flexibility do you have to reallocate budget?

8. Economic Viability & Market Factors

Points to Extract:
- Market conditions and economic outlook
- Competitive pressure and urgency
- Opportunity cost of not transforming
- Alternative investment options considered
- Economic sensitivity and scenario planning
- Long-term strategic value beyond immediate ROI

Sample Questions:
- How do current market conditions affect this investment?
- What's the competitive pressure or urgency?
- What's the cost of not doing this transformation?
- What alternative investments did you consider?
- How sensitive is this to economic changes?
- What's the long-term strategic value beyond immediate ROI?

Scoring Guidance

High Readiness: Adequate approved budget, realistic cost modeling, clear ROI case, committed resources, strong financial governance, strategic value recognized

Medium Readiness: Reasonable budget, basic cost estimates, positive ROI expected, resources identified, standard financial controls, business case approved

Low Readiness: Insufficient budget, poor cost understanding, unclear ROI, resource constraints, weak financial controls, questionable viability
